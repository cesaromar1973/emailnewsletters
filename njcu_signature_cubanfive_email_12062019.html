Who Were the Cuban Five?

The Cuban Five were Cuban revolutionaries and undercover operatives who were on a mission in Miami, beginning in 1990, to monitor the actions of Miami-based terrorist groups, that were planning and carrYing out bombings and other terrorist acts in Cuba aiming to bring (and did bring) death and destruction to the island, actions which were tolerated by the US government.

The Cuban Five were unjustly imprisoned in the United States after being arrested by the FBI on Sept. 12, 1998. They were framed-up and convicted in a biased and rigged US Federal Court in Miami, in an atmosphere of whipped-up “anti-Castro” prejudices. It was a political prosecution by the U.S. government; the Five were falsely accused by the U.S. government of committing espionage conspiracy against the United States.

As the Cuban Five pointed out in their defense, their objective was simply to prevent further acts of terrorism. And in fact their heroic work prevented many attacks and saved many lives. The campaign for their release became an international cause célèbre that brought together defenders of human and civil rights from all over the US and the world. On December 17th, 2014 ALL of the Cuban 5 Heroes returned victoriously to Cuba! Ramón Labañino, Antonio Guerrero, Gerardo Hernández, Fernando González and René González were finally reunited with their families and the people of Cuba, after spending over 16 years held unjustly in U.S. prisons for defending Cuba against U.S.-backed terrorism.

This special event will celebrate the 5th anniversary of this historic victory and its lessons for our struggles today as we face Washington's Deepening Anti-Cuban measures today!
